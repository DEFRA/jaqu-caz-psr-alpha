National overview

There are currently two Charging Clean Air Zones. These are Leeds and Birmingham.

Leeds

Leeds operates a CAZ B, meaning that it only charges non-compliant lorries, buses and Taxi/PHvs. For more information about CAZ types, click here.

Leeds' boundary includes anything within the: A6120, A647, B6154, M621, A61, A63 and M1.
This can be visualised on Leeds' Clean Air Zone boundary map.

For more information about the Leeds' Clean Air Zone, visit their site.

Birmingham

Birmingham operates a CAZ D, meaning that it charges non-compliant cars, vans, lorries, buses and Taxi/PHVs. For more information about CAZ types, click here.

Birminghamâ€™s Clean Air Zone will cover all the roads within the A4540 Middleway Ring Road, but not the Middleway itself.
This can be visualised on Birmingham's Clean Air Zone boundary map.

For more information about the Birmingham Clean Air Zone, visit their site.
